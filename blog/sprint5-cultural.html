Core Reflection


What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I'm just as happy working by myself as with others.

No matter how much I tell myself not to, I always get a flash of "Of crap, I'm falling behind!" every time someone shares work which is ahead of me.

The meditation stuff I've been learning on the Calm app has taught me that I can use metathinking to bounce myself out of any destructive thought patterns and get back on track. That is exremely powerful.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Learning requires self awareness, particularly in the form of a growth mindset and acceptance of the fact that you don't know what you don't know, and that's fine. Getting caught in negative thought processes and a fixed mindset serves to slow your learning progress, which can in turn contribute further to a fixed mindset in a vicious cycle.

Knowing yourself and how you approach problems, and recognising when to ask for help is important when working to a time constraint, as will often be the case when working in a team environment or on a project which is not self-driven.

Empathy is a very important ingredient in effective teamwork, and approaching all situations from a place of kindness and genuine desire to reach a mutually beneficial solution leads to a culture of trust and close co-operation. From a values perspective, everybody is human and deserves to feel supported when they need help, and from a pragmatic perspective, if someone asks for help and feels attacked or like a burden, they are less likely to ask for help in the future and will be less productive as a result.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Honestly, the power of meditation. I'd always assumed it wasn't for me, but dang. It's super handy for retaining focus, and keeping cool when things get frustrating.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Codifying my core values. I guess I'd always thought of my values as a kind of a reaction to a situation, mutable depending on other factors, but it was interesting to learn that I do have some overarching values, and also that they were so obvious to my friends and family.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

In my optinion, focusing on conecpts like neuroplasticity and growth mindset are crucial when we are being put into a position where we need to absorb a lot of information extremely quickly and in a high pressure environment. And working on soft skills will make us more employable at the end of the course, which for most of us is the ultimate goal.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead?

Not in the slightest. The time spent learning core has made the time spent practicing coding more productive.
