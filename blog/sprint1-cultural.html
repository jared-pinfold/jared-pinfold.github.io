Identity, Values and Strengths


How I see myself

My values:

I hold kindness and empathy in high regard, as well as allowing people to be their authentic selves. Further to this, I value honesty very highly, and encourage honest communication. I hate it when I feel like I'm being manipulated (compliment sandwich, obvious sales techniques etc)

I have a strong sense of justice when it comes to recognition for effort. Eg. Pay disparity, being taken for granted. People should be allowed a healthy work/life balance.

I love to collaborate, and love mentoring people too. I'm a helper. I like to help others achieve their goals.

My strengths

Communicator
Coach
Building rapport
Problem solving / Innovating

My Limitations

Lower than average self esteem
Willingness to help others sometimes affects my own time management
Outspoken if I think something is wrong (especially if I think it's a lie)

How my culture has influenced my values and identity

I'm a straight, white male from a middle class family. I have an awareness (and still a lot of blind spots, I'm sure) of the privilege that brings in our current society, and I always strive to be mindful of that and I try to be as inclusive and supportive of people as I can. I'm not especially versed in political science, but I identify as a left-leaning person, and I believe all people should be given opportunities and should be rewarded for contributing to society as best they can, not just for contributing to some rich person's yacht fund.

Describe an ethical decision I had to make

I was (still technically am) a manager of a retail store. I was at a meeting with the two owners of the business just after the first Covid lockdown and we were discussing hiring a new staff member, and the general consensus was that it was a risky proposition due to the uncertainty around the economy at the time. One of the owners started saying things like "There are plenty of people to hire right now, and we can pay them minimum wage, as a lot of people have lost jobs and are desperate", and phrases beginning with "Legally we don't have to..". I said that I believed he was being exploitative and that this method of hiring was not going to end up with a staff member who was ideally suited to the role or who would be motivated to stay long enough to become truly effective in the role.

Using provocative words like "expolitative" are obviously a bad idea when trying to get your point of view across without making the other person defensive, especially to your boss, but I was a bit upset at the idea of treating employees like a disposable resource, and I just kind of blurted it out. I ended up getting banned from high level meetings going forward. So yeah, lesson learned there, hopefully.

Evaluating my strengths and limitations in terms of my learning and career development

This is all in a Customer Service context. I tend to learn quickly when I first start a job because I enjoy learning how systems work, and then I start on trying to optimise them. I quickly become the subject matter expert and (oftern informally) become a mentor to other staff members. Often my willingess to drop everything to help someone gets me in trouble further down the line with my own workflow, so it's a personal journey of mine to work on time management and saying no.

Career development-wise, I've been made redundant 3 times and each time have ended up having to take another entry-level customer service job, I suspect ultimately due to not having the self confidence to apply for more advanced jobs or to interview especially well.

I've been working a lot on my self esteem issues and that's led me to coming to Dev Academy as I've found the desire to invest in myself.

Identifying which of my strengths might help me in my learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles

Problem solving, as the life of a programer is about understanding and solving problems. Coaching and building rapport, as I know that explaining a concept to someone aids in solidifying it in my own mind as well as helping the other person so it's win-win. I don't regard asking for help to be a weakness, so hope to be a positive influence on any people who might be shy about asking for help.

An example from my experience of where I was trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension

At my retail job we work closely with a sister company who operate our shared website and fulfil online orders. I don't have visibility of their system or internal processes, so I often ask questions or request that the web team perform little tasks for our mutual benefit. The manager of that team is often very passive-agressive and will respond with belittling comments etc. My response to that is generally to remind him that I'm not trained in the same system as he is, explain the context of my request more fully through the lens of that missing information, and that normally works. I also try to internally empathise with him to make his manner less hurtful/irritating, as I happen to know he suffers from depression and so do I so I know how that can sometimes distort social interactions.

From plum.io - a recruitment service including a strengths assessment

1. Coach
Your objective is to develop people's potential. Contrary to what others might think, you believe that every person has the potential for development. None of the people have achieved the ultimate level of excellence - there is always space to grow. You perceive it as a personal mission to help others utilize their potential and to experience success. As the result, you look for ways to facilitate their learning process - from challenging their thoughts in a discussion to creating environments which would facilitate learning process. You are one of those leaders that really care about the development of team members and they really appreciate it.

2. Empathiser
Your objective is to be empathetic to others' emotions. No one can step into the others' shoes better than you - it comes so naturally to you. You might not agree with every perspective and emotion, but most importantly you are able to understand what the person is going though. It allows you to have personalized approach to everyone, to see their differences and specialties, to include them and to treat fairly. Naturally, it draws others to you, as you know exactly how they feel. In a team, such a strength based on kindness can be essential for mitigating conflicts by making sure all team members are aware of each other's emotions and challenges.

3. Brainstormer
Your objective is to come up with new concepts and ideas. It's not even your objective - it's your way of life. You are constantly on the lookout to connect unconnectable things and to find new perspectives on familiar challenges. Whenever a new idea comes into your mind, you literally lit up like a light bulb. New angles, approaches and perspectives no matter how contrary or bizarre give you an endless source of energy. As the result, the others might see you as an innovative person willing to turn the world around and resort to you if they need some 'out of the box' ideas. You are a clear source of creative juices in any team.

4. Strategist
Your objective is to see the big picture. Have you heard about 'taking the helicopter view'? In your case, you are constantly on this helicopter! That's not even a skill, it's a special way of seeing things - a special perspective on the world at large, where everything is interconnected. While others could be confused by your short-terms steps, in your mind you have a clear multi-step strategy on how to arrive at the desired outcome and to avoid paths leading nowhere. Such a strategist is an essential asset for any team determined to find a clear direction in a complex situation.

5. Commander
Your objective is to take charge and direct people. In any situation, you enjoy bringing yourself forward to lead the team. Moreover, you don't come empty handed - by that time your opinion and views are formed and you can't wait to align others with you. Contrary to others, in this process, you are not afraid of confrontation when presenting your views. At the contrary, you feel that the confrontation and the open discussion is the first step towards an agreement. People who have a clear vision and can take a stand for it always draw followers, so will you. If you also master listening to others, this strength will help you to be a good leader without fear of confrontation.
